Kain tayo

Kain Tayo means, "Lets Eat?". In the Philippines anyone who's eating will say "Kain Tayo?" to invite anyone in the room to eat and share a meal. This website is a collection of beloved Filipino dishes that i grew-up with and now i'm sharing this to my family here in Germany. Living away from my family in the Philippines makes me feel homesick most of the time. Filipino food brings me comfort and connects me to my hometown.

Did you know?

Random Fun facts about Filipino Food that you may not know about.

Balut

Duck embryos are considered a delicacy. The balut is one of the most talked about street food in the Philippines. It's a developing bird embryo that is boiled and best enjoyed with a dash of rock salt or spicy vinegar. Albeit very intimidating at first, eating balut is actually quite pleasant, and once people get over the grossness factor, they usually go back for seconds.
